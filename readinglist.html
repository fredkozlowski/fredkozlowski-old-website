My Reading List

What I Want To Read

Buffett: The Making of an American Capitalist - Recommended by Byrne Hobart.

Coup d'État: A Practical Handbook by Edward Luttwak - Recommended by Byrne Hobart, plus it seems like a good follow up to Rules for Rulers by CGP Grey which I enjoyed.

Nicholas Nassim Taleb - The amount of noise his work generates is impressive. It seems that people think either he's very right or very wrong and they tend to be vocal about their opinion.

Things Hidden Since the Foundation of the World by René Girard - Recommended by Peter Thiel, specifically want to read to finish up an article about Peter Thiel's personal philosophy. Also interesting interplay with the following book.

Aztecs: An Interpretation by Inga Clendinnen - I read her article when posted on Hacker News, which made the Aztec worldview make so much more sense.

Snow Crash by Neil Stephenson - One of my favorite books ever was Cryptonomicon so I'm not really sure how I've avoided reading this book for this long. Very overdue especially since I love cyberpunk stuff.

What I'm Reading Currently

From Third World to First: Lee Kuan Yew - Absolutely fascinating so far. Very impressed by how matter of factly he describes creating a nation.

The Discovery of France - It's been very difficult for me to accept what I've been reading in this book, just like with Braudel's work. I believe it, but it's tough to think that a nation could create overseas colonies and yet face such challenges internally.

What I've Read (Or Failed To Read)

The Precisionists - Big F, was very light on technical detail. This video basically covers all the interesting material in the book.
